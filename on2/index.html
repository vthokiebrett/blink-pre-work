02
Your Partner Organizations
The youth organizations who co-brand ON2 and bring it to their community
~4 min
2.1Describe the ideal partner organization for ON2. What makes one a strong fit? ↗ Think this through first

Your three pilots are different entry points: CatchersU (national training), Arlington Storm (competitive travel), Arlington Babe Ruth (community league). What do the best-fit organizations have in common, identity, community, a committed family base?

2.2What does an organization need from a merch partner to say yes and to promote ON2 to its families?

The Team Up Athletics lesson is that organizations want a partner who understands their program and handles the details. The relationship is the unlock. What matters most to them, and what makes them hesitate?

2.3From the organization's side, what has to be true for a pilot to count as a success?

Think from the organization's side: revenue share, families actually buying and wearing it, the org looking good to its community. What does “this worked” look like for them?

03
Your Families & Kids
The kids who wear ON2 off the field and the parents who buy it
~3 min
3.1Describe the family and kid ON2 is for. Where are they in their youth-sports journey, and what do they care about?

Include the kid (age, sport, how they see themselves) and the parent (what they'll spend on and why). These families already invest real time, money, and commitment in their kid's athletic life.

3.2Why does a kid want to wear ON2 off the field, and why does a parent buy it?

The gap ON2 fills is off-field lifestyle apparel that travels from the diamond to the car ride home to school. What feeling or identity makes them choose it?

04
Your Content & Capacity
What you can produce and who will make it, pre-launch
~6 min
4.1Since ON2 is pre-launch, what have you built or validated so far? ↗ Look this up first

Pre-launch is completely fine. List whatever exists: signed or interested pilots, a following, an email list or waitlist, even if early or informal. If there's nothing formal yet, say so.

4.2For the co-branded collections, will partner organizations co-create the content, or will ON2 produce it all?

Think about who makes the photos, video, and posts that promote each organization's collection, ON2's team, the organization, or both. Be honest about what's realistic.

4.3Who will create ON2's content, and how many hours per week are realistically available? ↗ Think this through first

List who's involved: you, a collaborator, a freelancer, an editor. Be honest about actual availability, not what you wish it were. Your team brings deep brand-activation and operational experience, so tell us how that translates to weekly hours.

4.4What content formats feel most natural or important, and which do you want to avoid?

e.g. product and lifestyle photography, short-form video from tournaments, organization spotlights, founder storytelling, the sticker and touchpoint moments. Be honest about what you'll actually sustain.

05
Your Goals for Discovery
What you want to walk away with
~2 min
5.1What are your top 1–2 priorities for ON2 over the next 12 months?

e.g. proving the co-brand model across the three pilots, launching the off-field kid line, building a repeatable playbook for the next organizations, laying groundwork for the parent apparel line.

5.2What is the single most important thing you want to leave the Discovery session with?

e.g. a clear content strategy, a partner-acquisition playbook, positioning against the named competitors, a launch plan for the pilots, clarity on what to actually post.
